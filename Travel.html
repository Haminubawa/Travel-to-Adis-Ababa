Adis Ababa

Addis Ababa, Ethiopia's capital, is a vibrant and historical city often called the "Capital of Africa" because it hosts the headquarters of the African Union and many international organizations. Nestled at an altitude of around 2,355 meters (7,726 feet), it’s one of the highest capital cities in the world, which gives it a mild, temperate climate all year round. The city is a fascinating blend of old and new: modern skyscrapers and bustling markets sit alongside ancient churches and cultural landmarks. Highlights include the National Museum of Ethiopia, where the famous 3.2-million-year-old fossil, Lucy, is displayed, and Merkato Market, the largest open-air market in Africa. Addis Ababa is also famous for its coffee culture. Ethiopia is the birthplace of coffee, and in Addis, you can experience traditional Ethiopian coffee ceremonies—a ritual that’s both social and deeply cherished. Addis Ababa offers a gateway to Ethiopian history, culture, and traditions, making it a must-visit destination for anyone interested in Africa’s rich heritage.

Bole International Airport

Bole International Airport, located in Addis Ababa, is Ethiopia's main airport and one of the busiest in Africa. Officially known as Addis Ababa Bole International Airport (IATA: ADD), it serves as a major hub for Ethiopian Airlines, the country’s flag carrier and one of Africa's largest and most reputable airlines. The airport connects Ethiopia to destinations worldwide, including cities across Africa, Europe, Asia, and the Americas. In recent years, Bole International Airport has undergone significant expansion, with a modernized terminal that includes increased passenger capacity, duty-free shops, restaurants, and lounges to provide travelers with a comfortable experience. Its efficient transit system makes it a popular layover for international travelers, connecting flights across continents. Bole is also known for showcasing Ethiopian culture and hospitality, often giving passengers a glimpse into the country's rich heritage right upon arrival.

Skylight Hotel

The Skylight Hotel in Addis Ababa is a luxurious five-star hotel located near Bole International Airport, making it convenient for both travelers in transit and those staying to explore the city. Owned by Ethiopian Airlines, the hotel is designed with modern elegance and equipped with top-tier amenities that cater to international standards. The hotel features over 370 well-appointed rooms, a large conference center, and a variety of dining options, including an impressive Chinese restaurant, international buffet, and Ethiopian cuisine options that showcase local flavors. Additionally, the Skylight Hotel has a beautiful outdoor pool, spa, and fitness center, providing guests with both relaxation and recreation. One unique aspect of Skylight Hotel is its close integration with Ethiopian Airlines, offering amenities and services specifically designed to support airline passengers, such as day-use rooms and meeting spaces for business travelers. The hotel embodies Ethiopian hospitality and gives guests a comfortable, high-quality experience with easy access to the city and airport.

Places to Visit in Adis Ababa

  • National Museum: A museum with a collection that some say is among the most important in sub-Saharan Africa
  • Arada: A district with many museums and campuses of Addis Ababa University
  • Kirkos: A central district with the National Palace, Africa Hall, and Meskel Square
  • Mount Entoto: A mountain
  • Mercato Market: Africa's largest open-air market
